How it works

Hold, speak, done.

The whole loop, start to finish. Pick your platform for the setup steps.

  1. 1

    Download and install

    Grab the Windows app from your dashboard and run it. If SmartScreen appears, choose More info, then Run anyway. It docks in the system tray.

  2. 2

    Paste your token

    Right-click the Voice Typer icon in the system tray and open Settings. Paste the access token from your dashboard to link the app to your account. There are no OS permissions to grant on Windows.

  3. 3

    Hold, speak, release

    Press and hold the hotkey (F9 by default, and you can rebind it in Settings). The badge ring turns green while recording. Speak, then release. It turns amber for a moment and your words paste at the cursor.

  1. 1

    Download and open

    Download the macOS disk image from your dashboard and drag the app to Applications. The first time, right-click the app and choose Open to get past Gatekeeper.

  2. 2

    Grant two permissions

    In System Settings, under Privacy and Security, enable Voice Typer for Microphone and Accessibility. Microphone lets it hear you. Accessibility lets it paste your text. That is all it needs.

  3. 3

    Paste your token

    Click the Voice Typer icon in the menu bar and open Settings. Paste the access token from your dashboard to link the app to your account.

  4. 4

    Hold, speak, release

    Press and hold the hotkey (F9 by default, rebindable in Settings). The badge ring turns green while recording. Speak, then release. It turns amber for a moment and your words paste where the cursor is.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

What is Voice Typer?

Voice Typer is a lightweight push-to-talk dictation app. You hold a key, speak, and release, and your words are typed in as text wherever the cursor is.

Which platforms does it support?

Voice Typer runs on Windows 10 and 11 and on macOS 12 or later. One account works on either platform, one device at a time.

How does it work?

Press and hold the hotkey, which is F9 by default and can be rebound in Settings. Speak while you hold it, then release. Your speech becomes text and pastes at the cursor in any app that accepts text.

How much does it cost?

There are two plans. The first is 2.50 US dollars per month for 150 minutes. The second is 5 US dollars per month for 350 minutes.

What permissions does macOS need?

On macOS, Voice Typer needs only Microphone and Accessibility. Microphone lets it hear you, and Accessibility lets it paste your text. On Windows there are no OS permissions to grant.

Which languages and apps does it work with?

Voice Typer transcribes many spoken languages and can mix them in a sentence. It works in any app that takes text, including editors, browsers, chat apps, and the Claude Code terminal.
